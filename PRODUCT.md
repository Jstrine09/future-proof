# Product

## Register

product

## Users

James — sole user, personal daily driver on desktop. Used throughout the day as a primary knowledge base: capturing notes, browsing past writing, and occasionally exploring datasets. The context is familiar and frequent, like returning to a well-worn desk.

## Product Purpose

JumzysNotes is a personal notes manager with markdown support, tagging, media attachments, and a dataset viewer. It exists to give James a private, fast, and comfortable place to write, remember, and explore. Success means it feels like *his* space — not a tool he's renting, but a place he inhabits.

## Brand Personality

Warm, personal, cozy. The app should feel inhabited — like a notebook left open on a desk, not a dashboard opened in a browser. References: Bear (warm, typographic, journal energy) and Obsidian (dense and powerful, but deeply personal). Not a product; a place.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: No cookie-cutter gradient stat cards, hero metrics, or blue-everything layouts. This is not a team tool.
- **Bloated productivity app**: No panel sprawl, nested sidebars, or Notion-style modularity. One person, one flow.

## Design Principles

1. **Inhabited, not deployed** — the UI should feel like it belongs to someone, not like software installed on a machine. Personality shows through texture, type choices, and warmth, not decorative chrome.
2. **Content is the surface** — when reading or writing, the UI recedes. Navigation and controls appear when needed and step back otherwise.
3. **Warmth over precision** — round over sharp, earthy over clinical, soft dark over harsh contrast. Comfort matters more than efficiency optics.
4. **Intentional density** — enough information to navigate confidently (dates, tags, previews), never enough to overwhelm. Obsidian power, Bear serenity.
5. **Coherent, not assembled** — the app is one continuous space, not a grid of cards. Layout should have rhythm and flow, not a modular grid of identical containers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Personal app, single user, no formal requirements. Basic readability and keyboard support are sufficient.
